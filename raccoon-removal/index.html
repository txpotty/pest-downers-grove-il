Noises in the Attic or Walls

One of the most common indicators of raccoons is hearing strange noises coming from your attic, walls, or chimney, especially at night. Raccoons are nocturnal, so you're likely to hear them moving around, scratching, growling, or even chittering after dark. These sounds can range from light scampering to heavy thudding, depending on what they are doing and where they are located. Younger raccoons, or pups, can also make distinct whining or crying sounds that might be mistaken for birds or other animals. These noises often become louder and more persistent as the den solidifies and activity increases. Hearing these sounds is a strong signal that something larger than a mouse or squirrel might be residing within your home. The sounds might also indicate the presence of a mother raccoon and her litter, which requires a specific and careful removal approach. Don't disregard unusual noises in your home at night; investigate the source or call a professional to do so safely. Squirrels might also make noises in the attic, but raccoon sounds are typically heavier and more distinct. Understanding the difference in sounds can help you determine the likely culprit. If the noises are consistent and sound heavy, it's highly likely you have a raccoon problem. These sounds are often most noticeable in the quiet hours after sundown. A professional inspection can accurately identify the source of the noises and confirm whether raccoons are present. This is a critical step before attempting any removal, as misidentification can lead to ineffective or even dangerous actions. We have the expertise to differentiate between various types of wildlife noises and accurately diagnose the problem. Relying on auditory cues alone can sometimes be misleading, but combined with other signs, it provides a strong indication of a raccoon presence. The presence of raccoons, especially a family, can lead to surprisingly loud and disruptive noises. The sounds might change over time as young raccoons grow and become more active. Pay close attention to the timing and nature of the noises you hear to help our technicians pinpoint the location of the raccoons. Disturbing nesting raccoons, especially with young, can lead to aggressive behavior from the mother, highlighting the importance of professional intervention. Hearing these noises is often the first undeniable sign that you have a wildlife intrusion. It's a clear indication that you need to look into squirrel removal or raccoon removal from your property.

Damage to Exterior Property

Raccoons are incredibly strong for their size and can cause significant damage to the exterior of your home or business in their search for food and shelter. Look for signs of chewing, scratching, or tearing on your roof, siding, fascia boards, and vents. They may pull up shingles to gain access to an attic or tear open garbage bags and containers to find food. Damaged soffits are a common entry point for raccoons, as the material is often not as sturdy as other parts of the roofline. Chimney caps that are missing or damaged provide an easy pathway into your home's chimney system. Vents, especially those for the attic or dryer, can be ripped open if they are not properly secured. Don't underestimate a raccoon's ability to pry open or tear through various materials; they are persistent and resourceful when they want something. Inspecting your property regularly for these signs of damage is a good preventative measure. If you notice new damage that you can't explain, it's worth investigating further. The extent of the damage can provide clues about how long the raccoons have been present and the size of the infestation. Repairing this damage is crucial not only for preventing future wildlife issues but also for maintaining the structural integrity of your home. Ignoring damaged areas can lead to water leaks and other problems. Our team can assess the damage and make recommendations for necessary repairs as part of our comprehensive service. We use durable materials to ensure our repairs offer long-lasting protection against wildlife intrusion. Look for bent or broken eaves and gutters, as raccoons can use these as leverage to access the roof. Claw marks on siding or trees near your house are also potential indicators of climbing activity. Even small holes or gaps around your foundation or porch can be exploited by raccoons. Addressing damaged areas promptly is key to preventing a rodent infestation or rodent control problem as well. Upset Trash Cans

Raccoons are notorious for raiding trash cans in search of an easy meal. If you find your garbage cans overturned, lids removed, and trash scattered around your property in the morning, raccoons are likely the culprits. They have dexterous paws and can easily manipulate lids and containers. This is often one of the first and most obvious signs of raccoon activity in a residential area. Securing your trash cans with tight-fitting lids or placing them in a secure location like a garage or shed can help deter them, but it may not always be enough if they are determined. The mess left behind can be unsanitary and attract other pests. Raccoon droppings may also be found near the disturbed trash area. This behavior is driven by their opportunistic feeding habits; they are always looking for the easiest source of food. While it might seem like a minor annoyance, consistent trash raiding can be a strong indicator that raccoons are regularly visiting your property and potentially looking for a place to den. Using animal-resistant trash cans can be an effective deterrent. However, for persistent raccoons, professional intervention may still be necessary. The presence of overturned trash is a clear signal that raccoons are active in your neighborhood and potentially targeting your property. Presence of Feces and Odor

Raccoon droppings, also known as scat, can be found in areas they frequent, such as attics, decks, patios, and near potential entry points. Raccoon scat is typically tubular, blunt at both ends, and may contain undigested food items like seeds or berries. It is important to exercise caution when dealing with raccoon feces, as it can contain raccoon roundworm eggs, which are harmful to humans and pets. A musty or strong odor may also be present, particularly in enclosed spaces like attics or crawl spaces where they have been nesting. The accumulation of droppings over time can lead to significant odor problems. The presence of latrines, which are communal defecation sites, is a strong indicator of a well-established raccoon presence. These latrines are often found on elevated surfaces like decks, roofs, or logs. The odor associated with raccoon activity can also be a result of urine and nesting materials. This odor can permeate your home and make the living environment unpleasant. Identifying and safely removing raccoon feces and contaminated materials is a crucial part of the cleanup process. Professional cleaning and disinfection are often necessary to safely remove biohazards and eliminate lingering odors. The odor can also attract other raccoons or pests, reinforcing the need for prompt cleanup. Don't attempt to clean up raccoon droppings without proper personal protective equipment, as the roundworm eggs can become airborne and inhaled. The distinctive odor is often one of the less visible but still powerful signs of a raccoon infestation. It's a signal that there's not just a transient visitor but likely a sustained presence. This odor can lead to an ant control problem. A persistent, unpleasant odor that you can't identify is worth investigating, especially if accompanied by other potential signs of wildlife. Our team is equipped to safely handle the cleanup and disinfection of areas contaminated by raccoon feces. Addressing the odor is just as important as removing the raccoons themselves for restoring a healthy living environment. These signs point towards a potentially serious and unsanitary situation that requires expert handling.

Visible Raccoon Sightings

While raccoons are primarily nocturnal, seeing them on your property during the daytime is not necessarily a sign of illness, though it can sometimes indicate they are desperate for food or have been disturbed. However, frequent sightings of raccoons, especially during daylight hours, or seeing multiple raccoons together, indicate a higher likelihood of an established presence or a den nearby. Seeing a mother raccoon with her young is a clear indication of a den site on or very near your property. If you see a raccoon acting aggressively or showing signs of illness (stumbling, disorientation), keep your distance and contact animal control. These behaviors could indicate rabies, a serious concern. Observing raccoons repeatedly in the same area suggests they are comfortable there and may be nesting or regularly foraging for food. Pay attention to where you see them entering or exiting areas of your property, as this can help identify potential entry points. While a single, brief sighting might just be a raccoon passing through, consistent sightings are a stronger indicator of a problem. Don't approach or attempt to feed raccoons, as this can lead to unwanted habituation and potential aggression. Pets Behaving Strangely

A sudden increase in barking at night directed towards the roof or chimney could be a sign of raccoon activity. Pets have a strong sense of smell and hearing, often detecting wildlife before humans do.
